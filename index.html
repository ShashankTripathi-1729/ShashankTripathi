01   About

Who I am

I'm an undergraduate researcher working at the intersection of power electronics and intelligent control systems — running two degrees simultaneously because one wasn't enough.

My work is about making power converters smarter. I research ways to remove the need for precise mathematical plant models and replace them with real-time, adaptive control that works even when the system changes — from SiC/GaN gate drivers to model-free MPC to hardware-in-the-loop validation.

Two review manuscripts are in preparation for IEEE Transactions and Elsevier journals. Also actively working on DC-DC converter control with experimental hardware validation planned.

02   Research

What I'm building

Active Gate Driver Technologies — Comprehensive Review & Analysis
IEEE / Elsevier · In Prep
Systematic review of active gate driving for Si MOSFETs, SiC MOSFETs, and GaN HEMTs. Covers dv/dt & di/dt shaping for EMI mitigation and switching loss optimization. Comparative analysis of open-loop, closed-loop, and adaptive AGD architectures. First draft complete.
SiC MOSFETsGaN HEMTsEMI MitigationSwitching LossAdaptive Controldv/dt Control
Model-Free MPC — Methods & Applications in Power Electronics
Preparing Submission
Full taxonomy of MF-MPC frameworks eliminating dependency on explicit plant models. Ultra-local models, data-driven linearization, and learning-based MPC for nonlinear power systems applied to motor drives and DC-DC converters under parameter uncertainty.
Model-Free ControlUltra-Local ModelsMotor DrivesDC-DC ConvertersData-Driven MPC
DC-DC Converter Optimization — Control-Oriented Modeling & Transient Analysis
Ongoing
Switching transient analysis for buck, boost, and buck-boost topologies in LTspice. State-space modeling with duty-ratio sensitivity in MATLAB/Simulink. Closed-loop stability, ripple analysis, load-adaptive control. Hardware validation planned.
